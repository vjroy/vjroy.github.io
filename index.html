Research

LLM Research

I co-authored a research paper with MIT graduate Roger Jin evaluating 13 large language models on geographic route-planning tasks. Our benchmark, RouteEval, tested over 10,000 generated routes to measure distance and waypoint accuracy across real-world conditions. The project deepened my understanding of how LLMs integrate reasoning and tool use, blending my interests in AI and applied data science.

Machine Learning

Credit Card Fraud Detection

During my summer at Columbia University, I studied data science and machine learning and linear algebra in an intensive program. For my final project, a classmate and I built a model to detect fraudulent credit card transactions using Python, pandas, and scikit-learn. The experience taught me how to apply algorithms to real-world data challenges and reinforced my passion for ethical, impactful AI.

Mobile App

SplitDecision

Noticing how my coaches recorded splits by hand, my friend and I created SplitDecision, an iOS app that digitizes workout data for our track and cross country teams. The app allows runners to instantly upload their splits while coaches can sort, analyze, and track performance over time. It's been used by over 100+ athletes at my school.
